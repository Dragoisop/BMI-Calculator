What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple screening tool that estimates body fatness by comparing your weight to your height. First introduced in the 1830s by Adolphe Quetelet, a Belgian mathematician, BMI has become a standard health metric used worldwide.

The formula is straightforward: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]².

BMI Categories

Category BMI Range Health Status
Underweight < 18.5 May indicate nutritional deficiencies
Normal weight 18.5 – 24.9 Generally associated with good health
Overweight 25.0 – 29.9 May increase health risks
Obese ≥ 30.0 Significantly increased health risks

Important Note

BMI doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat mass, and may not be accurate for athletes. It should not be used as the sole indicator of health. Always consult healthcare professionals for comprehensive health assessments.
